Carl Corder was a physics researcher at University of Nebraska Kearney from 2008 to 2011 with a primary interest in Optical Vortices:

An optical vortex is a screw dislocation in a light wave that carries angular momentum. Angular momentum acts to twist the beam of light around its propagation axis which produces a self cancelling effect and thus a region of zero intensity. In this region, the phase of the beam is no longer well defined, and we have a phase singularity.
